Inputs Needed To Match Form 1040

These are the fields that matter most for the return lines shown on your CA-prepared Form 1040.

Target Line Most Important Inputs Why It Matters
`Form 1040 line 11b` `Interest`, `Total dividends`, `Short-term gains / losses`, `Long-term gains / losses`, `Other ordinary income` These drive adjusted gross income.
`Form 1040 line 12e` `Itemized taxes / SALT`, `Itemized interest`, `Other itemized deductions`, `Standard deduction` This decides whether itemized deductions or the standard deduction is used.
`Form 1040 line 16` `Short-term gains / losses`, `Long-term gains / losses`, `Dividends taxed at lower rates` These decide how much income is taxed at ordinary rates versus lower capital-gain / qualified-dividend rates.
`Form 1040 line 17` `Tax-free bond interest that counts for AMT`, `Other AMT adjustments / preferences`, `Minimum-tax foreign tax credit`, `Itemized taxes / SALT` These drive the minimum-tax amount coming from Schedule 2 / Form 6251.
`Form 1040 line 20` `Foreign taxes paid`, `FTC carryover`, `Other nonrefundable credits` These determine the amount from Schedule 3 used against tax.
`Form 1040 line 23` `Expenses tied to investing`, `Other taxes besides NIIT`, plus the income fields above These determine NIIT and any other Schedule 2 taxes.
`Form 1040 lines 25-38` `Federal withholding`, `Other payments / refundable credits`, `Underpayment penalty` These determine refund versus amount due.
